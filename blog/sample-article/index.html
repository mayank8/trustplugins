5 Underrated Chrome Extensions for Privacy in 2026

In 2026, the internet has become more personalized than ever before. But with this personalization comes a price: constant tracking. While most users have heard of the big names in ad-blocking, there are several powerful, yet underrated, extensions that go above and beyond to protect your digital footprint.

Here are our top 5 picks for the most underrated privacy extensions you should be using today.

1. uBlock Origin (Advanced Mode)

Wait, is uBlock Origin underrated? For many, it's the gold standard. However, most users barely scratch the surface. By enabling "Medium Mode," you can block all third-party scripts and frames by default, giving you granular control over what runs on your machine.

"It's not just an ad blocker; it's a wide-spectrum content blocker."

2. Privacy Badger

Created by the EFF, Privacy Badger doesn't rely on blocklists. Instead, it learns to identify trackers based on their behavior. If it sees a domain tracking you across multiple sites, it automatically blocks it. It's the "set it and forget it" tool for privacy-conscious users.

3. LocalCDN

Every time you visit a site that loads a library like jQuery from a Google or Cloudflare CDN, those providers see your visit. LocalCDN intercepts these requests and serves the files locally from your computer, cutting off that tracking vector entirely.

4. ClearURL

Have you ever noticed those long strings of gibberish at the end of a product link (e.g., ?utm_source=...)? That's tracking. ClearURL automatically strips these parameters in the background, ensuring that your clicks don't leak information about how you found a link.

5. Cookie AutoDelete

Browsing in Incognito mode is great, but it can be a hassle. Cookie AutoDelete allows you to browse normally but automatically deletes any cookies from a tab the moment you close it. You can whitelist your favorite sites so you don't get logged out of everything.

Final Thoughts

Privacy isn't about hiding; it's about control. By using a combination of these lightweight, open-source tools, you can reclaim your digital space without sacrificing the utility of the modern web. Have a favorite privacy tool we missed? Let us know through our feedback page!
